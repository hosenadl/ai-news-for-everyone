AI Basics. Start here.

New to all this? These short explanations will get you up to speed. No tech background needed.

🤖

What is AI, really?

AI (Artificial Intelligence) is computer software that can do tasks that used to need a human — like writing, answering questions, or spotting patterns.

Think of it like this: a very fast assistant who has read millions of books and can help with almost any question — but who sometimes gets things wrong, so you still check their work.

💬

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is the most famous AI tool. You type a question in normal English, and it types back an answer — like texting with a knowledgeable helper.

Example: You could type "Write a friendly sign telling customers we close early on Sundays" and it writes one for you in seconds. Claude and Gemini are similar tools made by other companies.

🆓

Does it cost money?

Most AI tools have a free version that is plenty for getting started. Paid versions (usually around $20 a month) are faster and more capable, but you don't need one to begin.

Tip: Start with the free version. If you find yourself using it every day, then consider paying.

⚠️

Can I trust what AI says?

Mostly, but not always. AI sometimes states wrong information with full confidence — experts call this a "hallucination."

The golden rule: treat AI like a bright new employee. Great at drafts and ideas, but double-check anything important — prices, laws, medical advice, dates.

🔒

Is it safe to use?

Yes, using AI tools is safe for everyday tasks. Just follow one rule: don't type in passwords, bank details, or private customer information.

Also good to know: scammers now use AI to write convincing fake emails. Be extra careful with unexpected messages asking for money or passwords — even ones that sound personal.

📱

How do I try it?

Go to chatgpt.com or claude.ai in your web browser — the same way you'd visit any website. Create a free account, then just type like you're sending a text message.

First thing to try: "Explain what AI could do for a small [your type of business] in simple terms."

AI for your business.

You don't need to be a tech person. Here are real, simple ways small business owners are using AI today.

✍️

Writing made easy

Emails, ads, social media posts, product descriptions, customer replies — tell the AI what you want to say, and it writes a polished draft.

Try saying: "Write a warm thank-you email to a customer who has shopped with us for 10 years."

📋

Paperwork & admin

AI can summarize long documents, draft contracts to review with your lawyer, organize notes, and turn a rambling voice memo into a tidy to-do list.

Try saying: "Summarize this letter in 3 bullet points" (then paste the letter in).

💡

A thinking partner

Stuck on a decision? Ask AI to list the pros and cons, suggest ideas for a slow season, or explain a confusing government form.

Try saying: "Give me 10 low-cost ideas to bring more customers into my bakery in winter."

🌍

Reaching more customers

AI can translate your menu or flyer into other languages, write posts for Facebook, and help you answer online reviews professionally — even the unfair ones.

Try saying: "Help me write a calm, professional reply to this negative review" (then paste it in).

The honest truth

You don't need to understand how AI works to benefit from it — just like you don't need to know how an engine works to drive a car. Start with one small task, like writing an email. That's how everyone starts.

Scam Watch.

Criminals now use AI too — and their tricks are getting very convincing. Here are the ones going around right now, and how to stay safe. Please share this with friends.

📞

The fake family phone call

Scammers can copy a person's voice from a short video clip online. Then they call you sounding exactly like your child or grandchild: "I'm in trouble, I need money fast, don't tell anyone."

Protect yourself: Hang up and call the family member back on the number you already have for them. Even better — agree on a family code word for real emergencies.

✉️

The too-perfect email

Scam emails used to be full of spelling mistakes. AI now writes them flawlessly — they can look exactly like your bank, the post office, or a supplier asking to "update payment details."

Protect yourself: Never click links in unexpected emails. Go to the company's website yourself, the way you usually do, or phone them using the number on your card or statement.

🎥

The fake celebrity video

AI can make a video of a famous person "recommending" an investment or miracle product — mouth movements, voice and all. These are fakes designed to take your money.

Protect yourself: Treat any video promising easy money as fake, full stop. Real investments are never advertised through celebrity videos on social media.

🔎

The fake customer service number

Scammers put fake "support phone numbers" on the internet for banks and airlines. You search, call the number, and a convincing "agent" asks for your card details or remote access to your computer.

Protect yourself: Get phone numbers only from the back of your bank card, a paper statement, or the company's official app — not from a web search.

The three golden rules

1) No real bank, government office, or company ever asks for gift cards or a money transfer to "fix" something. 2) Scammers always create urgency — real organizations let you take your time. 3) When in doubt, hang up and check with someone you trust. Being careful is never rude.
